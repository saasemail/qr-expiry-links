Private Sharing

How to share private links that expire and stay in control

When you share something personal online — a document, a message, or a private link — it often stays accessible longer than you expect.

Even after it’s no longer relevant, anyone with the link can still open it or pass it along.

The problem

Once you share a private link:

  • It stays active indefinitely.
  • Anyone with the link can keep accessing it.
  • It can be forwarded without your control.
  • You have no way to limit access over time.

What was meant to be temporary…

Becomes permanently accessible.

You lose control over something that was meant to be private.

The solution

TempQR lets you create expiring links and QR codes for private sharing.

You decide how long the link should work — and once time is up, it automatically expires.

This is useful for personal sharing, including sharing family photos with expiring links or sending private files as temporary document sharing.

  • No need to delete anything.
  • No need to track access.
  • No risk of old links staying active.

How it works

Creating a private expiring link takes just a few seconds:

  1. Paste the link you want to share.
  2. Set the expiration time.
  3. Click “Create Expiring Link”.

That’s it. Your link will only work for the time you choose — then it stops.

Who is this for?

This is especially useful for:

  • Anyone sharing personal or sensitive links.
  • Sending private documents or files.
  • Sharing something that shouldn’t stay online.
  • Temporary communication or one-time access.
  • Anyone who values privacy and control.

Real-life example

You send a private link to someone.

They open it and get what they need.

Later, the link automatically expires.

Even if it was saved or shared, it no longer works.

Your content was available only when it needed to be.
